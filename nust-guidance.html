Section 5

Frequently Asked Questions

Concise answers to common questions about NUST merit positions, selection lists, hostel admission, and planning.

A merit position is similar to the position you'd get in your class based on your marks. NUST calculates your aggregate using: 10% SSC/Matric, 15% HSSC/FSc, and 75% NET score. Students are then ranked according to their aggregate — the student with the highest aggregate gets Merit Position #1, the next highest gets Merit Position #2, and so on. Merit positions are assigned separately for different categories: Engineering, Computing, NBS & Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, Applied Sciences, Law (LLB), and Architecture — each program has its own separate merit position list.

Once the complete NET-4 result is announced, NUST usually releases the Merit List within 24-72 hours, showing your merit position(s) and last year's closing merit position(s) for the programs you applied to. After the merit list is announced, NUST normally gives a final opportunity to change your preference list, usually for 24-48 hours — after this deadline, the preference list is locked. The 1st Selection List is then announced within the next few days, and students generally get 3-5 working days to submit their dues after being selected. Subsequent selection lists are usually announced with a gap of 3-7 days. Since orientation is typically expected in the first week of September, NUST usually announces 4-6 selection lists before orientation, based on previous years' patterns.

As soon as your name appears in a selection list, the hostel application form becomes available on your admission portal. Hostel allotments are generally made on a first-come, first-served basis, so it's advisable to apply as early as possible after being selected.

Over the last few years, several factors caused significant changes in closing merits of popular programs: an increase in available seats, introduction of new Engineering and Computing programs, changes in the NET pattern for pre-medical students applying to Computing programs, inclusion of pre-medical students in Engineering programs without requiring additional Mathematics, and significant fee increases. These factors caused large fluctuations, with some programs changing by hundreds or even thousands of merit positions. For upcoming admission cycles, if no major policy changes occur, the probability of large deviation in closing merit positions is generally lower than in years with major changes.

Based on recent trends, it's reasonable to assume that this year's closing merit positions may remain relatively close to last year's — though this does NOT mean they'll be exactly the same. For example: if last year's closing merit position for a program was 1000, and your merit position is below 900, your chances of selection are generally considered quite good — but this is only a rough estimate, not a guarantee.

Regardless of how strong your chances appear, always keep 2-3 backup university options and do not rely solely on NUST until you have a confirmed admission offer. If your NUST admission is uncertain and you receive an offer elsewhere, it's often safer to pay the other university's dues while waiting for NUST's selection lists — if you later get into NUST, you can switch and apply for a refund under the other university's policy. Waiting only for NUST is risky, since missing both opportunities could cost you a gap year, which is usually a far greater loss than a few thousand rupees in provisional dues.

This guidance is based on general NUST admission trends and past patterns — not official NUST policy. Please verify time-sensitive details (like exact selection list dates and merit calculations) on NUST's official admissions portal at ugadmissions.nust.edu.pk.
